SEASONAL DRESSING GUIDE

SPRING
Spring is a season of transition -- the challenge is unpredictable temperatures that swing between cold mornings and warm afternoons. The best spring outfits blend both truths: a warm version of winter plus a cold version of summer. Lightweight layers that can be removed are essential: a denim jacket, a light blazer, a cardigan thrown over a floral dress or simple tee. Pastels and fresh whites feel seasonally right -- lavender, mint, peach, baby pink, sky blue. White trainers re-enter the rotation. Fabrics should be breathable but not yet full summer weight: cotton, linen, and rayon allow airflow while handling cooler moments. Floral prints, light-wash denim, and sneakers or ballet flats are spring staples. If an outfit feels too heavy, swap one piece for its seasonal opposite -- white jeans instead of cords, a light jacket instead of a wool coat.

SUMMER
Summer dressing prioritizes breathable fabrics above all. Natural fibers -- 100% cotton, linen, and light silk -- allow maximum airflow and absorb sweat. Loose silhouettes allow airflow; fitted styles should be in lightweight materials only. Lighter colors reflect heat; darker colors absorb it, so save your navy and black for evenings. Maxi dresses, cotton dresses, shorts, tank tops, and sundresses are everyday summer staples. Sandals and minimal footwear keep the look appropriately light. Linen blazers work for elevated summer looks without adding weight. Wide-brimmed hats and sunglasses are both functional and stylish. Layer lightly where needed: a sheer kimono over a tank top, or a light shirt over a dress for air-conditioned environments.

AUTUMN
Autumn is the best season for layering and one of the most creative for dressing. Build outfits in layers: a fitted base (tee or thin knit), a mid layer (overshirt, light sweater, or flannel), and an outer layer (jacket or coat). Earth tones dominate the autumn palette -- rust, olive, mustard, burgundy, camel, burnt orange, chocolate brown. These hues echo the season and work naturally together. Chunky knit sweaters, corduroy trousers, wool skirts, and denim jackets are autumn fundamentals. Ankle boots, knee-high boots, and loafers anchor autumn outfits. Textures like wool, flannel, corduroy, and leather add depth and warmth to looks. Scarves are both functional and stylish in autumn -- a plaid or knit scarf adds a layer and personality.

WINTER
Winter dressing is about warmth without bulk through smart layering. A thin thermal or close-fitting base layer means outer layers can remain slim and fitted. Coats are the focal point of winter outfits -- invest in one quality coat that works with most of your wardrobe. A well-chosen coat in a neutral (camel, black, grey, navy) carries the whole look. Knits add texture and warmth; cashmere and wool are the most luxurious options but any quality knit works. Dark washes and deep colors feel seasonally right in winter: charcoal, navy, deep green, amethyst, wine. Sweater dresses paired with tights and boots are versatile and comfortable for winter. Heavier cotton fabrics like denim and twill are appropriate for trousers and jackets. Boots -- ankle, knee-high, or over-the-knee -- are winter footwear staples.

TRANSITIONAL DRESSING
Transitional dressing -- the shifts between seasons -- is about building a wardrobe of pieces that work across multiple seasons with small adjustments. A midi dress works in summer with sandals and in winter with tights, boots, and a sweater. Dark wash jeans are appropriate in every season, paired with sneakers in summer or boots in winter. A trench coat is the ideal transitional outer layer: it handles spring rain, summer breezes, and autumn winds, and layers well over a chunky sweater in early winter. A medium-weight cardigan thrown over summer pieces extends their life into autumn. These transitional pieces reduce over-purchasing and build a smarter, more versatile wardrobe.

FABRIC GUIDE BY SEASON
Spring: cotton, linen, rayon, light denim. Summer: 100% cotton, linen, light silk, jersey, breathable synthetics. Autumn: flannel, fleece, wool, corduroy, heavier denim, cashmere. Winter: cashmere, wool, tweed, heavy knit, thermal fabrics, faux fur. Knowing which fabrics belong to which season makes getting dressed easier and ensures you are comfortable in every weather condition.

ACCESSORIES BY SEASON
Spring: lightweight scarves, floral headbands, pastel-toned bags, white trainers. Summer: wide-brimmed hats, sunglasses, beach totes, minimal sandal footwear. Autumn: woollen hats, leather gloves, plaid scarves, ankle boots, structured leather bags. Winter: knit hats, insulated gloves, cozy wraps, knee-high boots, dark leather bags. Seasonal accessories signal intentionality and tie an outfit to its context.
